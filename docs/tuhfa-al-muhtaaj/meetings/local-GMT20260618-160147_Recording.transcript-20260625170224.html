Ijāra for Worship Acts: Jihad, Prayer Leadership, Quran Recitation & The Prophet's Grave — June 18, 2026

📅 2026-06-18 🕐 16:01

Overview

This Tuḥfat al-Muḥtāj session opens with a preliminary exchange on a contemporary question (touching a smartphone Quran app without wuḍūʾ), then enters the main text's chapter on ijāra for acts of worship — specifically those for which hiring is invalid because they require personal niyya, versus those where hiring is valid. The session closes with a Q&A exchange on the conditions for wiping over khuff (leather socks).

Preliminary: Touching a Smartphone with a Quran App Without Wuḍūʾ

A scholarly exchange on whether it is prohibited (ḥarām) under the Shāfiʿī school to touch a smartphone that has a Quran application without ritual purity. Arguments for permissibility:

1. Cut-and-paste text is not kitāba in the ʿurf sense. A note in Ḥāshiyat ʿAbd al-Ḥamīd states that Quranic text formed by cutting and pasting letters is not considered "writing" (kitāba ʿurfan), and thus does not trigger the prohibition on touching. The argument: a phone's pixels constitute a display, not an inscription.

2. Analogy with tafsir books. Tafsir books may be touched without wuḍūʾ because they are not called "Quran" — the commentary predominates. By analogy, a phone is not called a muṣḥaf.

3. Default permissibility (barāʾat al-dhimma). Prohibition requires a positive legal basis. The default is permissibility; those who hold the act prohibited carry the burden of proof.

4. Scholars' positions: Ḥabīb ʿUmar ibn Ḥāmid al-Jaylānī and Shaykh Muḥammad al-Khaṭīb leaned toward prohibition on grounds of taʿẓīm (reverence). Ḥabīb ʿUmar ibn Ḥafīẓ and Sayyid Ḥusayn al-Saqqāf leaned toward permissibility. It was also reported that the Syrian Mufti Shaykh Usāma al-Rifāʿī had revised an earlier prohibition fatwa.

Main Lesson: Ijāra for Acts of Worship

The General Rule

Hiring (ijāra) is invalid for any act of worship that requires a personal intention (niyya). The rationale: the purpose of such worship is to test the mukallaf through personal obedience and self-mortification (kasr al-nafs). No substitute can fulfill this purpose. The agent (ajīr) therefore earns no wage even if he performs the act while expecting payment, because kullu mā lā yaṣiḥḥu al-istijār lahu lā ujra li-fāʿilih wa-in ʿamila ṭāmiʿan.

Jihad

Hiring a Muslim to perform jihad is invalid. Reasoning: when the enemy is present, jihad becomes personally obligatory (farḍ ʿayn) on those present; the hired Muslim's action occurs for himself, not as an agent. A dhimmī may be hired for jihad — but only by the Imam — since the dhimmī has no personal obligation of jihad.

Prayer Leadership (Imāma)

Hiring for prayer leadership is invalid, even for supererogatory prayers (nawāfil), because the imām performs the prayer for himself. The benefit of congregational prayer (faḍīlat al-jamāʿa) cannot be transferred to a mustaʾjir through a hire contract. Khutba (the sermon) is analogous: it requires personal performance. The author of the Tuḥfa notes the rājiḥ is that khutba differs from imāma in certain sub-rulings.

Adhān

The adhān does not require a niyya, so hiring for it is valid. The wage covers the full act together with observing the prayer time (maʿa naw raʿāyat al-waqt).

Visiting the Prophet's Grave ﷺ

Three categories of acts at the grave, each with its own ruling:

1. The visit itself (al-ziyāra li-l-wuqūf wa-l-mushāhada): Invalid for both ijāra and jiʿāla. The visit requires niyya. Al-Māwardī and others explicitly held this. The preferred view (al-rājiḥ) follows al-Māwardī over Abū ʿAbd Allāh al-Aṣbaḥī who held the opposite.

2. Supplication (duʿāʾ) at the grave: Valid for ijāra if the specific content of the duʿāʾ is stipulated in the contract (ʿayyanu mā yadʿū bih). If the content is not specified, ijāra is invalid but a jiʿāla is valid, since supplication admits of delegation (taqbalu al-niyāba).

3. Conveying the salutation (iblag al-salām): Both ijāra and jiʿāla are valid. Conveying a greeting is a form of agency that clearly admits of substitution.

Quran recitation at the grave, with duʿāʾ afterward: Valid, whether or not the reward is dedicated to the mustaʾjir, and whether or not he is physically present. The grave is a site of baraka and the descent of mercy.

Dedicating recitation/reward to the Prophet ﷺ: Permissible — indeed mustahabb — to dedicate the reward (thawāb) of one's recitation to the Prophet ﷺ, augmenting his honor. Affirmed by a group of later scholars. The Prophet ﷺ receives a share of the reward of every act of obedience performed by his entire umma.

Teaching and Teaching Quran

Hiring for Quran instruction is valid, on the basis of the authentic hadith: "Inna aḥaqqa mā akhadhtum ʿalayhi ajran Kitāb Allāh". Teaching other sciences: Valid only if the specific student and the specific subject are identified in the contract.

Q&A: Conditions for Wiping over Khuff

(with Tashfeen Ekram)

Why does the duration differ between traveler (3 days/nights) and resident (1 day/night)? The traveler's practical reliance on footwear is greater. A full three days accounts for the heavier footwear demands of travel.

Strength requirement (imkān mutābaat al-mashy): The khuff must be strong enough to permit continuous walking (yumkinu mutābaat al-mashy ʿalayhi). A woven sock that does not block water does not satisfy the Shāfiʿī condition.

Certainty vs. conjecture: Since wiping over khuff is a legal concession (rukhṣa), its conditions must be met with certainty (yaqīn), not mere conjecture — just as one may not shorten prayers (qaṣr) unless certain the minimum travel distance has been reached.

Assessment at each wearing: The strength condition is assessed at each individual wearing, not at the time of original purchase. Prayers performed after the socks became too weak are invalid and must be repeated — specifically, only those prayers about which the wearer is certain (yatayaqqan) that the khuff was already weakened at the time of performance.
