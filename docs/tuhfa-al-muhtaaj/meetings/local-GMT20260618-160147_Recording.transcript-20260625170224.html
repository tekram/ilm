Ijarah for Worship Acts: Jihad, Prayer, Quran Recitation & Visiting the Prophet's Grave - June 18, 2026

📅 2026-06-18 🕐 16:01

Overview

This Tuhfat al-Muhtaj session covers ijarah (hire/lease) as applied to acts of worship — distinguishing which worship acts may be hired out and which may not. Topics include: touching the Quran on a phone without wudu; hiring for jihad, prayer leadership, Quran recitation, visiting the Prophet's grave, and hajj/umrah by proxy; sending reward to the deceased; and conditions for valid leather socks (khuff) for wiping in wudu.

Key Rulings

  • Jihad: Cannot be hired out for Muslims; a dhimmi may be hired by the Imam only.
  • Prayer (including imamah): Cannot be hired out — the benefit accrues to the performer himself.
  • Adhan: May be hired out (no intention required).
  • Visiting the Prophet's grave: Cannot be hired; but dua at the grave and delivering salam can be hired or made subject to a ji'ala.
  • Hajj/Umrah by proxy: Permissible for deceased or permanently incapacitated persons.
  • Quran recitation: Valid in four scenarios — at the grave, away from grave with dua, in the presence of the hirer, or while holding the hirer in mind.
  • Teaching Quran: Permissible even if obligatory on the teacher.
  • Quran app without wudu: Arguments for permissibility are stronger — the phone screen is not a mushaf in the technical sense.
