Ijarah for Nursing & Childcare, Physician Liability, Landlord Duties & Talfiq Q&A - June 25, 2026

📅 2026-06-25 🕐 16:00

Overview

This Tuhfat al-Muhtaj session continues the chapter on ijarah (hire/lease) covering the hire of women for nursing and childcare, the distinction between large and small hadana, mandatory materials in hire contracts, physician liability, and landlord vs. tenant obligations. The session closes with Q&A on lending to riba debtors, combining madhabs (talfiq) in prayer and witr, and menstrual rulings when switching madhabs mid-cycle.

1. Ijarah for Nursing and Childcare

A woman — free or enslaved, Muslim or non-Muslim — may be hired for nursing and childcare if trustworthy. Small hadana: holding the infant for nursing — subsumed in the nursing contract. Large hadana: comprehensive childcare (washing, oiling, swaddling) — entirely separate from nursing. Three things must be specified in the contract: duration, location, and identification of the child. The wet nurse must eat what increases milk and avoid what harms it; intercourse with her husband is suspended if it would harm the infant. If milk dries up mid-contract, only the nursing portion fails; childcare survives.

2. Mandatory Supplies in Hire Contracts

The stronger opinion: auxiliary materials are not owed by the hired party (ink by the scribe, thread by the tailor, kohl by the eye-doctor, etc.). However, al-Rafi‘i clarified that custom (‘urf) governs — if custom is disputed, the contract must specify who provides materials, otherwise it is void for gharar. This applies to dhimma hire only; in ‘ayn hire only the work is owed.

3. Physician Liability

A skilled physician (errors rare, even if self-taught) owes no liability for failure to heal — the contracted benefit is treatment, not cure. He is owed the agreed wage. If cure is stipulated as a condition, the contract is void. A ji‘ala is valid — reward paid only upon recovery. An unskilled physician bears liability for harm, earns no wage, and must return medication costs.

4. Landlord vs. Tenant Obligations

Landlord: deliver key, major repairs, snow from the outer roof (jamlun), reclaim from usurpers, defend against fire/plunder, deliver with cesspit empty. Tenant: snow from courtyard/usable roof, ordinary refuse, cesspit cleaning during occupancy. Wind-blown soil: neither party. The landlord cannot be compelled to repair but the tenant gains the right to cancel (khiyar).

5. Q&A

Lending to a riba debtor: Not permissible if not repentant and intending to open another interest account — this assists in ongoing sin.

Talfiq in prayer and witr: A traveler who prays ‘Isha shortened on the Hanafi madhab then prays witr as one rak‘a on the Shafi‘i madhab: apparently permissible, as these are two separate acts with separate intentions — analogous to wudu on Hanafi, prayer on Shafi‘i.

Woman switching madhabs mid-cycle: A woman on the Shafi‘i madhab until day 10, switching to Hanafi on day 11 (istihadha per Hanafi): she owes no makeup prayers for days on the Shafi‘i madhab — no conflict between the two applications.
