Ijarah: Conditions for Valid Benefits, Future Leases & Promoting Zionist Brands - May 07, 2026

📅 2026-05-07 🕐 16:01

Overview

This session continues the study of Tuhfat al-Muhtaj by Ibn Hajar al-Haytami, covering the final conditions required for valid hire/lease contracts (ijarah), rules around leasing future benefits, permissibility of alternating use of a riding animal, and a contemporary fatwa on promoting Israeli-manufactured goods. Two supplementary questions were addressed: coerced acceptance of Islam by a dhimmi and the validity of such conversion.

1. Conditions on the Benefit (Manfa'a) in Ijarah

Background

The text enumerates the conditions a leased benefit must satisfy for the contract to be valid.

Ruling

  • Quantifiable (mutaqawwama): The benefit must have recognised monetary value in Islamic law. Prohibited or trivially cheap benefits render the contract void.
  • Accrues to the lessee: The benefit must be received by the lessee, not by the lessor.
  • Not for extracting the asset as the primary object: If the contract's purpose is to take ownership of a physical asset, it is invalid.
  • Exception: Renting a wet nurse is valid because the milk is incidental; the core object is nursing care (hadana sughra).
  • Exception: Renting a canal or well for water is valid by necessity.
  • Preserves the asset: The benefit must be enjoyed without destroying the asset (e.g., hiring a candle for lighting is invalid).
  • Permissible, owned, purposeful: Standard conditions paralleling those in sales.

Disputed case - renting apples for scent

Ibn Hajar held that a single apple may not be rented for its scent (scent is not its primary purpose), but a quantity may be, per Shaykh al-Islam Zakariyya al-Ansari, al-Mughni, and al-Nihaya. He distinguished this from musk and basil, whose primary purpose is scent.

2. Hiring for a Single Non-Laborious Word

Ruling

  • Invalid to hire a vendor to say a single effortless phrase for fixed-price goods (e.g., "Come and buy!"), because the phrase carries no monetary value.
  • Ibn Hajar: If goods are variable in price (e.g., luxury items), a celebrity's single word can materially raise the price, making the hiring valid.
  • Al-Ramli/Nihaya position: Hiring for any effortless single word is invalid regardless of goods type.
  • If the phrase is repeatedly said until it causes effort, the worker is owed ujrat al-mithl (fair market wage), not the agreed amount — upheld over the Adhra'i's objection, because repetition is normally inseparable from effective promotion.

3. Skilled Single-Act Services

Ruling

  • Al-Ghazali (*al-Ihya'): A doctor who prescribes a unique cure in a single word may not charge for it (no effort involved).
  • Exception (al-Ghazali): A craftsman who straightens a bent sword with one expert strike may charge, because his expertise required years of arduous training.
  • Al-Baghawi disagreed on the craftsman case; al-Adhra'i sided with al-Ghazali; the Nihaya (Ibn Hajar) follows al-Ghazali's distinction.
  • By analogy: a scholar who spent extensive effort researching a novel question (e.g., ruling on Bitcoin) may charge for the answer.

4. Leasing Coins, Dogs, and Birds

Ruling

  • Coins (dirham/dinar): May be rented for decoration, weighing, or stamping — valid benefits. The contract must specify the purpose; without specification, the rental is void outright.
  • Coins fitted with a loop (mu'arra) become permissible jewellery; those merely pierced remain coins and require the decoration declaration.
  • Dogs and pigs: Renting them for hunting or guarding is invalid in the stronger view, because neither their bodies nor their benefits carry monetary value in Islamic law.
  • Birds (for their sound or colour): Al-Baghawi raised the khilaf; al-Mutawalli definitively held it valid, followed by al-Nihaya, al-Mughni, and al-Rawda.

5. Lessor's Ability to Deliver the Benefit

Ruling

  • The lessor must be able to deliver the benefit — both physically and legally.
  • Buildings erected in prohibited areas (e.g., Mina, 'Arafat — must be demolished) cannot be rented.
  • Runaway slave (abiq) or usurped property: Cannot be rented by someone who cannot immediately recover possession after contracting.
  • Iqta' land (ruler's land-grants): Per al-Suyuti's fatwa, may be rented by the grantee but the lease terminates upon the grantee's death, since the iqta' does not necessarily pass to heirs.

6. Hiring Menstruating Women, Married Women, and Nursing Mothers

Ruling

  • Menstruating or post-partum Muslim women may not be hired by ijarah al-'ayn to serve a mosque or teach Qur'an, because mosque service requires prolonged presence in the mosque, which is forbidden to them. Ijarah al-dhimma (abstract obligation) remains valid.
  • Married women may not be hired for work without the husband's permission.
  • Exception (al-Adhra'i): If the husband is absent or is a minor child, and the work will finish before his return or maturity, the hiring is valid.
  • The lessee has no right to prevent the husband's conjugal relations with a hired nursing wife, unless the relations would immediately harm the nursing infant.

7. Ijarah al-'Ayn vs. Ijarah al-Dhimma for Future Benefits

Ruling

  • Ijarah al-'ayn (hiring a specific person or asset): The benefit must commence immediately — deferred start is invalid.
  • Exception: Renewing a lease with the same tenant before the current term expires is valid, since the benefit is already in the lessee's possession.
  • Exception: Leasing a specific animal for Hajj at the time of the departing caravan is valid.
  • Ijarah al-dhimma (hiring an abstract obligation): Future delivery is valid, analogous to salam (forward sales).
  • Practical example discussed: reserving specific swimming pools months in advance (ijarah al-'ayn with deferred start) is problematic.

8. Alternating Use of a Riding Animal (Ta'aqub)

Ruling

  • Valid to rent a riding animal to two persons who take turns riding it on a journey. The contract must specify which portions each person rides.
  • If they dispute who rides first, they draw lots (qur'a).
  • Sleeping on the animal without the owner's consent is impermissible, because the sleeping person's dead weight burdens the animal.
  • Division may be by time (days) or distance (leagues); if neither a clear agreement nor a recognized custom exists, specification is required.

9. Contemporary Fatwa: Promoting an Israeli/Zionist Brand

Question

An eyewear shop employee was instructed to promote a newly introduced Israeli brand. Is promotion permissible, and does refusing make him derelict in his duty?

Ruling

  • If one has certainty or strong suspicion that money paid to this company funds prohibited acts (killing innocents, financing the occupation, weapons), then promoting it is haram.
  • If the company itself does nothing haram and there is no such suspicion, promotion is permissible with dislike (makruh).
  • If the products are manufactured on usurped (magh-sub) land (Palestinian land taken by force), this is an additional prohibition.
  • As an employee hired to sell all the shop's brands, he is obligated to follow reasonable employer directions unless they constitute a sin. He may quietly avoid promoting this brand without announcing his objection — unless it is clear the employer would not have hired him knowing this, in which case his silence amounts to deception (ghishsh).

10. Coerced Acceptance of Islam by a Dhimmi

Question

If a dhimmi is forced to accept Islam, is the conversion valid? If he later apostatises, does the hadd (capital punishment) apply?

Ruling

  • If the dhimmi was offered the choice of paying jizya but refused and then chose Islam, this is not coercion — his Islam is valid, and subsequent apostasy is subject to the hadd.
  • If the jizya option was not offered (forced to choose only between Islam and death), this is coercion — his Islam is invalid. He remains a non-Muslim; neither his "Islam" nor his subsequent "apostasy" has legal effect, and he is not buried with Muslims.
