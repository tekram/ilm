Iddah Rules for Twins - May 27, 2025

📅 2025-05-27 🕐 17:02

Topic

Postpartum bleeding (nifâs), intermittent bleeding after childbirth, continuous/irregular bleeding (istihādhah), related procedural rulings, and a few ancillary fiqh points (sujūd al‑sahw, sitting in prayer, prepaid ticket fees).

Key definitions & thresholds

  • Twins (al‑tawāmin): births counted as “twins” juristically if interval < 6 months. If ≥ 6 months → treated as two separate pregnancies (no dispute).
  • Nifâs: postpartum bleeding; Hanafi texts commonly work with a conventional upper limit of 60 days.
  • Haydh (menstruation): requires at least one day-and-night (yawm wa laylah) to be classified as menstrual bleeding.
  • Istihādhah / mustahadhah: continuous or irregular non‑menstrual bleeding; treated by special rules.
  • Dam faṣād: “corrupt” or pathological blood (technical category for bleeding neither clearly haydh nor clear nifâs).
  • Practical time markers used in the lesson: 1 day+night (min. for haydh), 15 days (threshold for treating an intervening purity as separating episodes), 60 days (conventional upper bound for nifâs).

Core legal problem (twins / staggered deliveries)

When births (twins) occur at different times and bleeding appears before and after the second delivery, how is nifâs attributed?

Classical positions (three)

1. Nifâs attributed to the first child — postpartum period begins at first birth.

2. Nifâs attributed to the last part delivered — count nifâs from the final expulsion (the lesson’s preferred Hanafi position; supported in chains attributed to Muḥammad and Zafar; narrations also from Aḥmad and Dāwud).

3. Each birth produces its own nifâs — each delivery may trigger an independent nifâs period.

Scholarly attributions (concise)

  • Hanafi preference taught in lesson: start nifâs at exit of last part (position linked to Muḥammad and Zafar).
  • Some jurists (Ibn al‑Qāsi, Imam al‑Haramayn, al‑Ghazali) held nifâs is from the first child.
  • Other jurists classify blood between births as either haydh or dam faṣād depending on circumstances.

Practical consequences (examples)

  • If each birth gives its own nifâs → potentially two full nifâs periods (e.g., up to 60 days after each).
  • If nifâs is only from the first child → later bleeding may be aggregated; if total > school’s nifâs‑limit (e.g., 60 days) it becomes mustahadhah or dam faṣād.
  • If nifâs counted from the last part (Hanafi preference) → official nifâs begins when the final fetal part is expelled; earlier bleeding is not the final nifâs.

Intermittent bleeding after childbirth (rules)

  • Two main treatments:
  • Treat the first postpartum blood as nifâs and later bleeding as haydh if intervening purity qualifies.
  • Treat all bleeding as one continuous nifâs (so the intervening purity does not separate episodes).
  • Quantitative test applied in lesson:
  • A purity interval of 15 days or more between bleeding episodes often qualifies as a separating tahāra allowing the later bleed to be classified as haydh.
  • If purity < 15 days, majority/juristic practice tends to treat the later bleed as still part of nifâs (or as dam faṣād).
  • Minimum for haydh: an episode must last at least a day-and-night to be counted as menstruation.

Long or complicated cases

  • If bleeding extends beyond the school’s nifâs limit (commonly 60 days):
  • Majority/author’s choice in class: treat post‑60 bleeding as haydh (apply menstrual rules thereafter).
  • Minority: treat entire 60 days as nifâs and anything after as istihādhah.
  • Partial expulsion of fetal tissue: jurists differ whether bleeding after a fragment is nifâs or not — consult madhhab and medical evidence.
  • General rule: if bleeding persists or totals exceed conventional limits, treat as mustahadhah and apply istihādah rules; seek medical and juristic counsel.

Practical rules for istihādhah / use of stuffing (tampons, pads) and bandaging

  • Internal stuffing (classical ḥashw) or modern tampon/pad that prevents outward flow is acceptable and can substitute for bandaging (taʿṣīb / talajjum).
  • Procedure recommended in lesson:
  • Clean the area before ritual purification.
  • Insert absorbent stuffing; secure with an outer bandage/strip (taʿṣīb) before making wudu.
  • Make wudu (or tayammum) after packing.
  • If bandage/tampon is dislodged or visible external blood appears → renew wudu before prayer.
  • For fasting: leaving internal stuffing in during the daytime is permitted in classical reports; avoid harm.
  • If bleeding continues despite proper packing and precautions, treat as istihādhah for ritual acts (renew wudu for each obligatory prayer in many positions; see madhhab practice).

Intention in wudu for a mustahadhah, timing, and prayer validity

  • Intention (niyyah): the soundest view taught — intend istibāhah (to make oneself permissible to pray). It is not required to phrase the niyyah as “lifting a past hadath.”
  • Wudu before prayer time: avoid performing wudu for a fard significantly before its time except for necessity; if made at start of time, pray promptly.
  • If bleeding stops and you wash/make wudu:
  • If bleeding resumes before finishing prayer (or you began in an impure state), the stronger view in lesson invalidates that prayer — repeat it.
  • If bleeding stops genuinely and you purified before entering prayer, prayer is valid unless bleeding resumes and you had time to renew wudu beforehand (then stronger view may require qāḍā).
  • If bleeding resumes during prayer: prevailing/stronger view — prayer invalid, repeat after purification.

Other related rulings (concise)

  • Touching the Muṣḥaf / reciting Qur’an:
  • Istihādhah woman: majority allow recitation and handling the Muṣḥaf; treat her permissibly for worship acts.
  • Haydh (menstruant): classical positions vary; many restrictions remain for haydh that do not apply to istihādhah.
  • Ghusl after nifâs: when postpartum bleeding stops, ghusl is required before resuming prayer/sexual relations.
  • Tampon/bandage removal/external blood: visible external blood or removal of dressing → renew wudu.
  • Sitting posture between prostrations (for those who cannot sit normally):
  • Permitted only for genuine/permitting hardship; do not change normal sitting except when severe inability or to preserve khushūʿ. Keep forehead not projecting beyond knees.
  • Sujud al‑sahw: consists of two prostrations. Omitting the second through clear error normally invalidates the prayer (must be repeated); if followers reasonably believed the imam intended to stop, they are excused.

Evidences & attributions

  • The recorded excerpt contained no explicit Qur’an or hadith citations; the lesson relied on classical juristic reasoning and author attributions.
  • Classical hadiths commonly used in this topic (not quoted in recording but present in literature): e.g., “When the menses begin, leave prayer; when it ends, wash and pray” (reported in Bukhari & Muslim); narrations on guidance for continuous bleeding in Abu Dawud/Tirmidhi. Jurists weigh chains and reconcile reports when forming practice.

Major points of disagreement (summary)

  • Whether nifâs begins from first birth, last part, or each birth (three classical positions).
  • Whether a brief post‑birth bleed under a day‑and‑night is nifâs, dam faṣād, or other — Hanafis (Abu Hanifa/Abu Yusuf) treat it as nifâs in many reports; others treat as dam faṣād.
  • Whether post‑60 days become haydh or remain nifâs with subsequent istihādhah treatment.
  • Frequency required to renew wudu for mustahadhah / continuous discharges and whether one purification covers multiple prayers.
  • Permissible sitting posture in prayer when mobility/comfort conflicts with correct form.

Practical checklist for action (concise)

  • 1) Determine whether births are “twins” (<6 months). If ≥6 months → treat as separate pregnancies.
  • 2) Decide which juridical approach you follow (your madhhab or local scholar): nifâs from first birth / last part / each birth — apply consistently.
  • 3) Check purity intervals after delivery:
  • Purity ≥ 15 days between bleeding episodes → may separate episodes (later bleed potentially haydh).
  • Purity < 15 days → treat later bleed as continuation of nifâs or dam faṣād per madhhab.
  • 4) If total postpartum bleeding approaches/exceeds 60 days → treat as mustahadhah and consult medical + juristic authority (many treat post‑60 as haydh; others not).
  • 5) For istihādhah: clean area, insert stuffing/tampon, bind (taʿṣīb), then make wudu before prayer; renew as required by your madhhab.
  • 6) If in doubt in complex cases (fetal fragment, long bleeding, legal consequences for ʿiddah / talaq) — obtain a case‑specific fatwa from a qualified jurist of your madhhab and seek medical assessment.

Administrative / ancillary rulings noted

  • Sujud as‑sahw: two prostrations — train imams and followers to avoid communal invalidation; repeat prayer if omission is clear.
  • Sitting between prostrations: allowed only for real hardship; otherwise maintain correct sitting posture.
  • Prepaid booking/change fees (non‑refundable small fee): treated as part of the ticket price / lawful commercial surcharge in ordinary cases — no inherent fiqh objection; check carrier terms if contractually contentious.
