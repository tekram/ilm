Waqf on Owned Irrigation Canal, Nazarship Precedence, Cosmetic Surgery & Women's Work - June 23, 2026

📅 2026-06-23 🕐 16:00

Overview

This session covers two classical fatawa on waqf law, followed by resolutions from the 18th and 19th Symposia of the Majma' al-Fiqh al-Islami bil-Hind: invalidity of endowing property for a privately-owned irrigation canal; founder's stipulated nazir takes precedence over government authority; educational loans and riba; cosmetic surgery; women working outside the home; divorce issued by non-Islamic courts; and prayer times at extreme latitudes.

1. Waqf on Privately-Owned Canal: Invalid

Void for two reasons: (1) it is a waqf on oneself — the Shafi'i school invalidates endowments benefiting the founder; (2) waqf for the repair of privately-owned property is invalid since the owner has no legal obligation to maintain it.

2. Stipulated Nazir Overrides Government Authority

As long as descendants are qualified, the government must restore nazarship to them. The stipulated nazir derives appointment from the founder; the founder's stipulation is like the lawgiver's stipulation. Lineage requires two witnesses, who may rely on widespread common knowledge.

3. Symposium Resolutions

  • Educational loans: Interest-bearing loans are prohibited; genuine necessity cases should consult a qualified mufti.
  • Cosmetic surgery: Permitted to correct defects; not permitted to reverse aging or for pure aesthetics.
  • Women's work: Permitted outside the home with guardian's permission and conditions (hijab, no seclusion, no overnight stays).
  • Divorce by non-Islamic courts: Valid if husband petitions; wife's unilateral petition not valid Islamically — she must seek khul' or faskh.
  • Prayer at extreme latitudes: If twilight never disappears, calculate by reference to nearest location where it does.
